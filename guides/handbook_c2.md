# C2 Proficiency — Teachers' Handbook: Marking Glossary & Additional Sample Answers

---

## Part 1 — Glossary of Key Marking Terms

### General

**Generally** — a qualifier meaning "not in every way or instance". Performance described as *generally appropriate* is not as strong as *appropriate*.

**Flexibility** — the ability to adapt language, organisational devices, or task conventions rather than repeating the same form. Flexibility evidences better control and a wider repertoire. It allows a candidate to better achieve communicative goals.

### Content

**Relevant** — related or relatable to required content points and/or task requirements.

**Target reader** — the hypothetical reader set up in the task (e.g. a magazine's readership, your English teacher).

**Informed** — the target reader is informed if content points and/or task requirements are addressed and appropriately developed. Some content points need little development (*state what X is*); others require it (*describe, explain*).

### Communicative Achievement

**Conventions of the communicative task** — genre, format, register and function. A personal letter should not be written as a formal report, should be laid out accordingly, and use the right tone.

**Holding the target reader's attention** — used in the positive sense: the quality of a text that allows a reader to derive meaning and not be distracted. Does NOT refer to texts that force close reading because they are difficult to follow.

**Communicative purpose** — the communicative requirements as set out in the task, e.g. *make a complaint*, *suggest alternatives*.

**Straightforward ideas** — limited, usually concrete subject matter requiring simpler rhetorical devices.

**Complex ideas** — more abstract nature, or wider subject area, requiring more rhetorical resources to bring together and express.

### Organisation

**Linking words** — high-frequency cohesive vocabulary: *and, but, because, first of all, finally*.

**Cohesive devices** — more sophisticated linking (*moreover, it may appear, as a result*) plus grammatical devices: reference pronouns, substitution (*There are two women. The one on the right…*), ellipsis, repetition.

**Organisational patterns** — less-explicit between-sentence and beyond-sentence connections: climactic sentence ordering, parallelism, rhetorical questions to introduce new paragraphs.

### Language

**Basic vocabulary** — survival/simple transaction vocabulary.

**Everyday vocabulary** — common non-technical vocabulary.

**Less common lexis** — vocabulary that appears less often in context; often helps express ideas more succinctly and precisely.

**Appropriacy of vocabulary** — words/phrases that fit the task context. *I'm very sensible to noise* → *sensible* should be *sensitive*. *Big snow* → should be *heavy snow* (collocation).

**Simple grammatical forms** — words, phrases, basic tenses, simple clauses.

**Complex grammatical forms** — noun clauses, relative/adverb clauses, subordination, passives, infinitives, verb patterns, modals, tense contrasts.

**Grammatical control** — the ability to consistently use grammar accurately and appropriately to convey intended meaning.

**Range** — the variety of words and grammatical forms used. Higher levels = greater variety of words, fixed phrases, collocations, grammatical forms.

**Overuse** — repeatedly using the same word due to insufficient repertoire. (Note: topic-forced repetition is not overuse.)

**Errors** — systematic mistakes.

**Slips** — non-systematic mistakes; the candidate has learned the item but made a one-off mistake. Where most other examples of a point are accurate, a mistake on that point is likely a slip.

**Impede communication** — getting in the way of meaning. *Meaning can still be determined* = some reader effort required.

## Part 2 — Additional Sample Answers with Official Scores

### Q1 — Part 1 Essay (Behavioural Change / Social Norms)

**Candidate A** — Essay arguing for tolerance across generations; mentions *cult of individuality*, *generation gap*.

| Subscale | Band | Commentary |
|---|---|---|
| Content | 4 | All content relevant; final key point not addressed. Target reader informed. |
| Communicative Achievement | 3 | Conventions used with sufficient flexibility. Register occasionally uneven (*rising thru the ranks, dizzy heights*). |
| Organisation | 3 | Well-organised; variety of cohesive devices (*In this connection, moreover, For example, Throughout the history of humanity*). *Despite that* incorrectly employed. |
| Language | 2 | Wide range including less common lexis (*deeply ingrained, perennial problem, quicker on the uptake, by leaps and bounds*). Grammatical and lexical errors present but do not impede communication. |

| Organisation | 4 | Well-organised; wide variety of cohesive devices (*So, by definition, However, Consequently, Yet, Whereas, Yet at the heart of the matter*). Clear paragraphing would have enhanced coherence further. |
| Language | 5 | Wide range including less common lexis with fluency, precision, sophistication (*in a sociological sense, individuality comes into play, critically reviewed, stifling, leapfrog towards*). Sophisticated grammar, fully controlled (*Just as… so it is for*). Inaccuracies only as slips. |
