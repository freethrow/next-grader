# C2 Proficiency Writing — Self-Assessment Checklist

## Four Assessment Subscales

**Content** — Has the candidate done what the task required?
- Completed the task, meeting all criteria and including all necessary information.
- Did not include everything required, or wrote something irrelevant / misinterpreted the task.

**Communicative Achievement** — Is the writing appropriate for the task?
- Used the right style and register for the genre; fulfils the communicative purpose(s).
- Writing isn't suitable for the purpose(s) or genre conventions; may not hold the reader's attention.

**Organisation** — Is the writing coherently structured?
- Ideas are logically ordered, clearly connected, and appropriately prioritised.
- Difficult for the reader to follow; organisational features may be inappropriate for the genre.

**Language** — Is the language accurate and varied?
- Good range of vocabulary and grammar, used accurately and appropriately. Even if some mistakes, the reader can still understand.
- Mistakes make the text difficult or confusing for the reader.

---

## Content Checklist

- Have I covered **all** the key information required by the task?
- Have I written **only** information relevant to the task?
- Have I developed the basic points with my own ideas?

## Communicative Achievement Checklist

- Have I achieved the main purpose(s) of the text (explaining, persuading, suggesting, comparing, etc.)?
- Have I used a suitable mix of fact and opinion?
- Have I used a suitable style and register (formal or informal) for the task?

## Organisation Checklist

- Have I used paragraphs appropriately to organise my ideas?
- Have I used genre-appropriate organisational features (titles, headings, openings, closings, etc.)?
- Is the connection between ideas clear and easy to follow (linking words, pronouns, etc.)?
- Are ideas balanced appropriately, with suitable attention given to each?

## Language Checklist

- Have I used a wide range of vocabulary?
- Have I avoided repeating the same words and phrases?
- Have I used a range of simple and more complex grammatical structures?
- Have I correctly used common phrases relevant to the specific task or topic?
- Is my use of grammar accurate?
- Is my spelling accurate?
